# Accurate, scalable, and fully automated inference of species trees from raw genome assemblies using ROADIES
** [DOI: 10.1016/j.ajhg.2019.03.012](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.250055312) ** 
** Read:** July 9, 2026

## Main Ideas
- ROADIES infers species trees directly from genome assemblies through an automatic pipeline
- Random sampling method helps overcome gene tree discordance, complex polyploidy, limited scope of premediated loci, gene annotation and whole genome alignment steps. 
- ROADIES produced a species-level normalized Robinson-Foulds distances of 0.038. All species assigned in phylogeny correctly
- ROADIES, with 100 drosophilid genomes, converged in 1105 minutes with 1627 gene trees
- GPUs can be leveraged to accelerate the pipeline when gene tree counts reach higher magnitudes (tens of thousands)
